Your product’s value and physical protection

Understanding where your product’s value actually comes from, and whether the physical layer of that value has any protection. This determines what a dupe can and cannot take from you before brand, visual identity, or legal tools are even considered.

Where does most of your product’s commercial value come from — the physical product itself (formulation, function, performance), the visual identity (look, packaging, design), or something less tangible (brand meaning, customer relationships, community, trust)?
Low
Primarily physical — customers buy it for what it does or how it performs. The product itself is the value.
Partial
Mixed — value sits across two or more of these layers genuinely and deliberately.
High
Primarily intangible — customers buy it for what the brand means, the relationship they have with it, or what it represents. The physical product is part of the value but not the core.
Does your product rely on a patent, trade secret, or proprietary process that creates a genuine technical barrier a competitor would struggle to overcome?
Low
No — no patent, trade secret, or proprietary process exists. Competitors can produce a functional equivalent without difficulty.
Partial
Some barrier exists — but it is partial, time-limited, or known to be vulnerable.
High
Yes — a patent, trade secret, or proprietary process creates a meaningful substitution barrier that is not easily overcome.
Does that technical story — the formula, the process, the science behind the product — actually drive consumers to choose your product over cheaper alternatives that claim to do the same thing?
This question matters even where no hard patent exists. A formulation narrative can do commercial work without legal protection behind it.
Low
No — consumers in this market treat competing products as functionally equivalent regardless of formulation claims. A cheaper alternative is an acceptable substitute for most of them.
Partial
Somewhat — a portion of customers factor the formulation or technical story into their purchasing decision, but it is not the dominant driver of preference.
High
Yes — customers actively seek out this product for its specific formulation or technical properties. The brand has built genuine conviction around what makes it different — Olaplex-style belief in the formula — that a cheaper alternative cannot easily displace.
